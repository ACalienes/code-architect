What "act on orders instantly" actually needs.

You asked if Pitch Deck has to be "brought on officially" to act on orders the moment they land. Short answer: that's not the lever. Three different things are getting bundled into one — here they are, untangled.

✅ Pitch Deck already acts on orders — today.

It's a live engine (running 3 days straight). It already watches its mesh inbox, grabs incoming work orders, does them, and marks them done. It does not need The Board to receive or act on orders. What you actually want — instant — is a separate piece called the push-nudge, and that's the real gap.

The three mechanisms (don't confuse them)

1
The order channel — live now

How an order actually reaches Pitch Deck. This is the mesh (the office's message system), and Pitch Deck already listens on it, claims the order, and works it. This is the part you thought was missing — it isn't.

speed today: checks every ~30 seconds → acts within 30s of an order landing
2
The instant trigger — the real gap

"Immediately" means not waiting out that 30-second check. That needs a push-nudge: the moment an order lands, something taps Pitch Deck on the shoulder to act right now instead of on its next check. This is your "generalize the NAMI nudge" item — and it's a mesh feature, completely separate from The Board.

speed after: order lands → acts in ~1 second
3
The Board (being "brought on officially") — context, not speed

This gives Pitch Deck shared awareness — it can see decisions, status, and briefs flowing across the office. Genuinely useful, but it's the coordination layer, not the trigger. And The Board also checks every 30s — so enrolling here would not make it instant.

speed: also ~30s · value: context & visibility, not reaction time

My recommendation

①
If "immediately" means "reliably within ~30s" → you already have it. Nothing to bring on. The thing actually worth checking is reliability (Pitch Deck is on the list of agents with a known silent-drop bug — that's the comms-fix, already Codex-reviewed and ready to apply).
②
If "immediately" means sub-second → build the push-nudge (your ASAP item). That's the lever — not Board enrollment. It's a mesh-contract change, so it gets the full review gate.
③
