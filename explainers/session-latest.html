What this is

Like any growing organization, the "who works here?" list lives in three different places for different purposes. (1) The static manifests under knowledge/manifests/ are the spec / job descriptions — what each agent IS and what it CAN do. (2) ~/.kameha/agents.json is the HR roster — who's currently employed, what's their inbox, are they active. (3) mesh.db.agents is the phone directory — who has a working mesh route, who can send to whom, what's the tier.

Why it matters to you

When all three agree (top 6 rows), the agent works normally. When they disagree, weird things happen: agents that work in practice but aren't in the spec (chronicle, conductor — no job description), agents whose spec says they exist but aren't on the HR roster (framer, acd — no employee badge), and one outright ghost (kmg — real code, zero paperwork). My own row is the worst: I claim I can call 9 other agents in my manifest, but the mesh phone directory has zero of those routes wired up. I am a paper tiger right now. Fixing this is most of the "owners.json bootstrap" → "mesh route seeding" work in W4+.

My read

For daily operations, this is fine — Kai + 5 active agents work. For "Code Architect actually does cross-agent work," every drift row above is blocking. The fix is mechanical (a one-time reconciliation pass once you authorize owners.json bootstrap). Highest priority once W4 ships is closing my own row.
