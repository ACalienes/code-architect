The Pending-Review Nudge

A post waiting for client approval can silently miss its scheduled slot — no publish, no warning. This job watches for that and pings you (and the client) once, before it's too late.

01 The problem, in one picture

Every post moves through this lifecycle. The publishing robot only ever picks up posts that are Approved or Scheduled. A post parked at Pending Review — waiting on the client — is invisible to it.

If the client never clicks approve, the post sits in Pending Review forever. The scheduled time comes and goes — nothing publishes, and nobody is told. That's the exact gap that nearly hit the DAG Memorial Day post.

02 The fix — a quiet hourly check

Once an hour, the job runs three steps. No new app, no new notification channel — it reuses what NAMI already has.

STEP 1

Find the stuck ones

Look for posts in Pending Review whose scheduled time is within the next 18 hours and hasn't passed yet — across all active clients.

STEP 2

Claim it once

Stamp the post as "nudged" in a single atomic step, so it can never be picked up twice — even by overlapping runs.

STEP 3

Send the nudge

Ping you (with the review link to forward) and, if the client has an email on file, gently re-ping the client too.

03 Two nudges, one trigger

You asked for visibility on both sides — so a single stuck post fires two messages.

The operator nudge

To you
  • Same email + bridge + SMS fanout as the approval alert you already get
  • Non-urgent — the slot hasn't passed, it's a heads-up
  • Carries the client review link so you can forward it directly
  • Leads with "You have content pending review" — clear at a glance

The client reminder

To the client (e.g. Dan)
  • Subject: "✅ You have content waiting for your approval"
  • Opens with "You have content pending your review and approval" — no ambiguity
  • A plain email to the client's address on file — no new channel
  • Links straight to their review page to approve
  • Toggle it off anytime with one setting if you'd rather stay operator-only

04 Why it can't spam

The worry with any hourly job: does it re-send every hour? No. One new column on the post (pending_nudge_sent_at) is the memory, and it's claimed before the message goes out.

-- one atomic step: stamp it, but only if it isn't already stamped
UPDATE posts SET pending_nudge_sent_at = now
WHERE id = ? AND pending_nudge_sent_at IS NULL;
If two runs ever overlap, only one wins the claim — the other sees the row already stamped and walks away. At most one nudge per post, guaranteed. (If the client later asks for a fresh round of changes, the stamp is cleared so a re-review gets a new nudge.)

06 Two things to know

The client reminder needs Dan's email allowed. NAMI only emails addresses on its safelist (so test runs can't spam real clients). For the client reminder to actually reach Dan, his address must be on NAMI_EMAIL_SAFELIST — otherwise it quietly skips and only you get nudged.
This is the durable fix, not tonight's rescue. It won't retroactively save tomorrow's 9am DAG post unless it's deployed today. For that one post, the reliable move is a one-off manual nudge now — forward Dan the review link directly.
