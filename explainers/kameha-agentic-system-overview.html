How they talk to each other

Every agent-to-agent message goes through a single canonical message bus. Each route is permissioned — who can talk to whom, at what trust tier, with what auto-deliver vs. approval policy. Messages carry idempotency keys, expiry timestamps, and correlation IDs. Every send is logged, every delivery is acknowledged. The result: agents stop talking to each other directly, and "who said what to whom" becomes auditable.
