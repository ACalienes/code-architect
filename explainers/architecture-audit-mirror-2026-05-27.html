Is the original plan still real?

You asked the right question before we build anything. The plan was: agents live headless on the Mini · you work on the laptop · everything mirrors to the Mini for safety. I audited all three claims against the live machines. Two hold. The third — the safety mirror — is broken, and has been for ~2.5 weeks.

The three claims, scored

✅
1 · Agents live headless on the Mini holds
22 processes running always-on under pm2 (mesh-api, Conductor, CFO, the Board trio, KMG…). The Mini genuinely is the dedicated, always-on home. This part of the design works.
✅

❌

The hourly sync that's supposed to keep the Mini current has never run successfully. The script lost its executable permission, so cron fails instantly every hour.

Two deeper truths the audit surfaced

The mirror was always git-only

Even when it ran, the sync was pull-only, covered 10 of 21 repos, and only ever moved committed + pushed code. Your large in-flight volume (CFO 50, DAGDC 63, Framer 122 uncommitted files) was never in scope — it can't mirror, by design.

The Mini isn't off-site safety

The Mini's hourly ~/.kameha backup commits locally only — no remote. It's version history on the same disk, not a copy somewhere else. If the Mini's drive fails, that backup fails with it. The real off-site safety net is GitHub, not the Mini.

So — pivot, or fix? The model is sound; the wiring is wrong

Don't abandon the concept — correct what "safety" means.

Headless-agents-on-the-Mini + laptop-workstation is a good design. The mistake was treating "the Mini mirrors me" as the safety story. It never could be — the Mini is a runtime host, not a backup. Safety is commit + push to GitHub. Once we name the layers correctly, each gets the right fix — and the Board "door" we were about to build is one of four distinct things, not a patch over a broken mirror.

① Source safety needs discipline
protecting your actual work

Commit + push to GitHub. That's the only real off-site copy. The big uncommitted volume on the laptop is protected only by your local Mac backup (Time Machine?) — not by this system.

② Mini currency broken — quick fix
keeping running agents up to date

Fix sync-repos.sh (make it executable, add the missing repos, handle feature branches). One small fix restarts the hourly pull. This is an operational need, not safety.

③ Runtime visibility the Board door
seeing what agents actually do

Option A. A separate layer entirely — git mirroring would never show "CFO drafted an invoice." Still the right next build, now correctly scoped as its own thing.

④ Runtime-state safety gap
the live databases

Give ~/.kameha a remote so the Board DB + conductor.db are pushed off the Mini. Today they live on one disk only.
